Problem Statements & Analysis

1. Driving Experience, Road Surface Conditions, and Educational Level

Age of Driver and Driving experience are influential factors in driving style that lead to accidents. Young drivers, recently learning to drive, are more careless and account for a major share of accidents due to overspeeding.

2. Factors Affecting Fatality

Days of the week and time of day influence accident counts. Commuters in a hurry during weekdays contribute to higher accident rates, especially in office areas.

Weather conditions have minimal overall effect, though normal and rainy conditions are notable causes.

3. Vehicle Involvement in Accidents

Driving discipline significantly impacts accident severity. Violating traffic rules leads to unnecessary incidents. Collisions with vehicles and roadside objects rank as the second most common accident factor.

4. Service Period and Ownership

Vehicles with delayed servicing are more prone to accidents. Regular maintenance significantly reduces accident rates.

5. Impact of Area and Road Design

Y-cross sections contribute to accidents due to poor visibility. Proper urban planning and road engineering can reduce accidents in urban and school areas.

6. Environmental and Light Conditions

Bad visibility combined with poor road conditions, like water or snow, creates dangerous situations. Accidents frequently occur during daylight hours under normal conditions.

Suggestions

  • Increase police checkpoints and patrols on weekends in high accident rate areas.
  • Introduce mandatory road safety programs in junior high schools.
  • Provide government incentives for regular vehicle servicing.
  • Encourage experienced drivers to attend defensive driving courses periodically.
  • Install road signs, streetlights, and accident-prone warnings at necessary locations.
  • Improve vehicle safety features like braking systems, tire quality, and airbags.
